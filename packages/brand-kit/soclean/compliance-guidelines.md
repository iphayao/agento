# SoClean — Compliance Guidelines

Compliance rules for all marketing content produced by or for SoClean. Applies to AI-generated, human-written, and agency-produced content.

---

## Core Compliance Principle

**Marketing language must accurately reflect what the product actually does and what can be verified.**

The burden of proof lies with the brand. Any claim that cannot be evidenced by:
- Product specification (2-ply, 180 sheets, etc.)
- Manufacturer test data
- Verified customer experience (attributed reviews)
- Regulatory certification

...must not be used.

---

## Claim Category Rules

### Category A — Always Approved (No Review Required)
Claims derived directly from verified product specifications.

| Example | Basis |
|---|---|
| 2-ply / 2 ชั้น | Product spec |
| 180 sheets / 180 แผ่น | Product spec |
| Pack of 5 / แพ็ค 5 กล่อง | Product spec |
| 50 packs per carton | Product spec |
| Suitable for face and hands | Intended use |
| Available on TikTok / Shopee / Lazada | Factual |

**Process:** No review required. Use directly.

---

### Category B — Approved with Attribution (Review on First Use)
Claims grounded in sensory experience or customer-reported outcomes.

| Example | Required attribution |
|---|---|
| เนียนนุ่ม | Product characteristic; acceptable as brand claim |
| ฝุ่นน้อย | Manufacturing intent; acceptable as positioning claim |
| ลูกค้าบอกว่านุ่ม | Must link to actual review data |
| ส่งเร็ว | Must reflect actual delivery performance |

**Process:** Marketing manager reviews on first use. If supported, add to approved list. Re-check if product formulation changes.

---

### Category C — Conditional (Case-by-Case Review)
Claims that could be interpreted as absolute or medical in nature.

| Example | Condition for use |
|---|---|
| "ไม่ระคายเคือง" | Only if supported by customer reviews with this language |
| "เหมาะสำหรับผิวบอบบาง" | Only if no allergen testing is implied; frame as comfort claim |
| "ฝุ่นน้อยกว่า" | Only if not implying a named competitor comparison |

**Process:** Must be reviewed by marketing manager before publication. Document the decision.

---

### Category D — Prohibited (Never Use)
See full list in `prohibited-claims.md`.

**Process:** AI system must flag and block. Human override requires documented justification and should be rare.

## Platform-Specific Compliance Notes

### Shopee and Lazada
- Both platforms have automated claim detection for health and safety language.
- Listings with "antibacterial", "hypoallergenic", or absolute health claims may be suppressed or rejected.
- Do not include any Category C or D language in product titles.
- Product descriptions should be Category A or Category B only.

### TikTok Shop
- TikTok's moderation algorithm flags health-adjacent content.
- Avoid any visual or verbal implication of medical or antibacterial properties.
- "ฝุ่นน้อย" and "เนียนนุ่ม" are safe. "ฆ่าเชื้อโรค" or "ปลอดภัยทางการแพทย์" will cause video suppression.

### Facebook Ads
- Meta prohibits health claims that imply treatment or cure.
- Compliance reviewers at Meta may flag: "safe for sensitive skin" (hypoallergenic implication).
- Stay strictly within Category A and B language in ad copy.

## Agento AI Content Review Workflow

When Agento generates content, it must go through this compliance gate:

```
AI generates draft content
        ↓
Automated prohibited-term scan
(match against prohibited-claims.md term list)
        ↓
If flagged → Content held as DRAFT
           → Flag shown to editor with specific term highlighted
           → Suggested alternative from approved-claims.md provided
        ↓
If clean → Content marked DRAFT, ready for human editorial review
        ↓
Human editor reviews → APPROVED or REJECTED
        ↓
APPROVED content → Available for publishing
```

**Rule:** All AI-generated content starts as DRAFT. No AI content should auto-publish.

## What to Do When a Claim is Disputed

If a customer, competitor, or platform disputes a claim:

1. Immediately pause content containing the disputed claim.
2. Review against this document.
3. If claim is in Category D → remove permanently, document incident.
4. If claim is in Category B/C → gather evidence; if evidence insufficient, retire the claim.
5. If claim is Category A (spec-based) → verify the specification; update if needed.
6. Document the outcome and update this file if policy changes.
